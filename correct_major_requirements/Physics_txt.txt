physics major
advising
department website

effective fall 2026

advising

students with any questions about courses or majors in physics should speak with physics department advisors.

students wishing to pursue a minor in physics must develop a specific plan for its completion in consultation with a physics advisor.

advising appointments can be scheduled in the physics student services office, 1440 randall lab [(734) 764-5539] or through the physics advising website.

grade policies

students must earn at least a c-in all courses they plan to include in the major, including the prerequisites.

prerequisites

students prepare for the study of physics by completing the following introductory sequences (four courses total):

one of the following 100 level lectures:

physics 140: general physics i (should be elected concurrently with physics 141)
physics 160: honors physics i (should be elected concurrently with physics 141 or 161)

one of the following 100 level labs:

physics 141: elementary laboratory i (should be elected concurrently with physics 140 or 160)
physics 161: honors introductory mechanics lab (should be elected concurrently with physics 160) 

one of the following 200 level lectures: 

physics 240: general physics ii (should be elected concurrently with physics 241)
physics 260: honors physics ii (should be elected concurrently with physics 241 or 261) 

one of the following 200 level labs:

physics 241: elementary laboratory i (should be elected concurrently with physics 240 or 260)
physics 261: honors electricity and magnetism lab (should be elected concurrently with physics 260) 

students pursuing a physics major should also pursue the calculus course sequence as they progress in their physics courses. students are expected to have a strong understanding of calculus up through multivariate calculus, differential equations, and linear algebra. the recommended math courses are:

math 115 or 185
math 116 or 186
math 215 or 285
math 216, 286, or 316

test credit (ap, ib, etc.) does not fulfill any requirements for the physics major. students with advanced preparation in physics are strongly encouraged to elect the honors introductory sequence (physics 160, 161, 260, and 261).

it is not necessary to complete all courses in the prerequisite sequences in order to declare the physics major. students are encouraged to officially declare as soon as they make the decision.

requirements
minimum credits: 35

once a student has mastered introductory content and declared the major, they prepare for advanced study of the discipline by fulfilling the intermediate courses requirement (three courses), then move on to master advanced concepts, skills, problems, and topics of the discipline by completing the advanced courses requirement (five courses), and elective requirement (four courses).

intermediate courses requirement
students must complete all of the following:

physics 316: mathematical methods for physics & engineering
physics 390: introduction to modern physics 
physics 391: introduction to modern physics lab 

ideally, physics 316 is elected no later than the second semester of the sophomore year. physics 390 and 391 can be elected concurrently with physics 401 or 405 (part of the advanced courses requirement).

advanced courses requirement
students must complete all of the following:

physics 401: intermediate mechanics
physics 405: intermediate electricity and magnetism
physics 406: statistical and thermal physics
physics 441: advanced laboratory i
physics 453: quantum mechanics

students are encouraged to complete both physics 401 and 405 before electing physics 453.

elective requirement
students round out their study of physics by taking a minimum of four elective courses (totaling at least twelve credits) of the choices listed below. note that any course used in the intermediate and/or advanced course requirements cannot also fulfill an elective.

physics 340: waves, heat, and light
physics 360: honors physics iii
physics 380: intermediate topics in physics, section titled "introduction to quantum computing" (note: other topics taught as physics 380 are not automatically included in the major; see an advisor)
cmplxsys 335: networks
any physics course numbered at the 400- or 500-level, excluding physics 501, 517, 518 and 590
constraints

courses elected as pass/fail may not be included in the major.

students may use a maximum of three credits of independent study, such as physics 415, 495, 496, or 497, towards the major requirements. this includes students pursuing an honors major. honors majors should not elect physics 495 as it does not fulfill any requirements for the honors major. 

residency

a minimum of 15 credits at the 300 level or above must be completed in residence. courses elected on the ann arbor campus or at off-campus sites directed by ann arbor faculty are considered in residence. this includes stdabrd, camp davis, biological station, and michigan-in-washington.

distribution policy

no course used to fulfill a major requirement may be used toward the lsa distribution requirement. in addition, courses in the physics subject area may not be used toward the distribution requirement.

honors

highly motivated students who have an interest in conducting independent research are encouraged to complete an honors major. in addition to the requirements for major, honors majors must complete an additional six physics credits numbered at the 400 level or above (excluding physics 415, 501, 517, 518 and 590), maintain a cumulative gpa of 3.4 or above, and produce a senior thesis under the supervision of a faculty member. honors students have the option to enroll in physics 496 and/or 497 if they wish to earn credit for their research. they should not enroll in physics 495 as it does not fulfill any requirements for honors major. students seeking to complete an honors senior thesis with a faculty member outside the physics department must also secure a co-advisor from within physics. for more information, including important deadlines, visit the physics honors website.
